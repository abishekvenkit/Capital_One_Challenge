Trip Type


    To the left we see a comparison of the trip types taken by riders. It is evident 
    that the majority of trips are one way, at an overwhelming 90.3%. This means that 
    often times, there will be a net change in the location of bikes after a day. We will 
    examine this more in our analysis of the change of bikes over the course of the day, 
    and we will see how we can ensure that there are enough bikes in each area of the city.

Most Used Bikes


    To the right we can see an analysis of bike usage. We can see that the most
    used bikes have been rode for over 250 hours! That is quite a lot of wear 
    and tear. This indicates that there may be some quality control issues for 
    certain stops or areas. This also might mean that the company should invest
    in more bikes. Once a bike hits the 200 hour mark, it should be flagged for 
    maintenance or retired. We can even use this data, in coordination with 
    our data on the most visited stops, to ensure our best bikes are serving 
    the most people.

Trip Duration vs. Start Time


    To the left we can see a comparison between the average duration of a ride and the time 
    of day. What is interesting about this comparison is the stark difference between durations. 
    During very late hours of the night, we see a very high trip duration. This could be caused 
    by lost riders, irresponsible riders, or those who need to travel far distances to get home. 
    Once we reach the regular day hours, we see a significant drop in the duration, which 
    hovers around 25 minutes per ride.
