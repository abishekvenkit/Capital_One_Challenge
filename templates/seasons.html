Season vs. Pass Type


***NOTE: A different data set was used for these charts because the orginal
set did not have sufficient data for the Spring to retrieve reliable results.
The data used can be found in the README.

    To the left we see a comparison of the seasons with the pass type. Our results
    support much of the other data we have seen in that the majority of riders are 
    monthly users. For this visualization, and the two below, it was necessary to 
    import extra data from the meto-bike-share website in order to get a good picture.
    The original data set was missing data for the month of May and June completely,
    making it difficult to see what happens in the Spring. By taking a complete new
    set of full year data, we can take out any skew from combining the sets. We end 
    up seeing that Spring and Summer are very popular for monthly riders, and this 
    makes sense considering the pleasant weather.

Number of Rides per Month


    To the right we can see an analysis of bike usage per month. Using the
    imported data from the website allows us to see a very sensible progression
    in the ridership over the course of the year. We can see that winter months 
    have much lower bike usage than Spring and Summer months.

Trip Duration vs. Month


    To the left we can see a comparison between the average duration of a ride and the month.
    Similar to the comparison in ridership, we see a sensible relationship between weather
    and duration. In the warmer months, rides are typically longer. The peak duration is almost
    an hour during late summer to early fall. Again, the use of our full-year data set gives us
    a strong representation of changes over the seasons.
